### 2. Constitution Inclusion

The omission is the better state; do not add the constitution.

**Reasoning:**
The core requirement is to prevent "process-heavy critiques" by focusing the verifier on the concrete work (`diff`) vs. the concrete task (`spec`). The constitution, like the process manual, contains abstract principles separate from the direct task. Including it re-introduces the risk of dilution and encourages critiques based on procedural or philosophical alignment rather than task completion. Adhering to the "prefer removal over addition" principle, the current, leaner state better serves the requirement's intent, even if it deviates from its literal text. The spec's allow-list should be amended to remove the constitution.
